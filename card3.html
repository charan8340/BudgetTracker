50/30/20 Budget :

                   One of the most common types of percentage-based budgets is the 50/30/20 rule. The idea is to divide your income into three categories, spending 50% on needs, 30% on wants, and 20% on savings. This way, you set aside a fixed amount from your income for each of the categories. This reduces your urge to withdraw amounts from one category for another.Its Straightforward budget for beginners.

Effeciency :

While its not as detailed as other budgets, the 50/30/20 rule provides a clear guideline for allocating income to essentials, discretionary spending, and savings, making it easy to follow.

Suitable for :

People who prefer a more relaxed approach to budgeting and dont want to micromanage every expense.
